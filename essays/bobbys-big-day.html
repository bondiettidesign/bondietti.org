Bobby’s Big Day

February 2024

It’s a jeans and rain jacket kind of day outside, but the weather hasn’t stopped anyone from going out. The man who hangs out in front of the corner market has chosen lovers-rock as the neighborhood’s morning soundtrack. Bobby squeegees the rain off of a bench and plops down to wait for the bus, pre-game Red Bull and samosa in hand.

Off the bus, downtown and on the way to the river, he slips into a crowded thoroughfare to check his phone. Dylan and his gang are late. This news suits Bobby just fine, for inside this very thoroughfare, begging to be tasted, are tiny, picture perfect, pink donuts in cute little cardboard boxes.

Before the empty donut box ever sees the bottom of a bin, Bobby’s head is involuntarily and violently whipped around by the power of his very own left nostril as it directs the rest of his body towards the distinctive smell of smoky teriyaki. A tiny kawaii girl in gigantic foam shoes steps out from behind another food counter and she is serving a sandwich that looks like a piece of art. He makes a mental note of this blackened chicken teriyaki sandwich and plans on swinging back around in a few, but it’s time to get to the river market where friends and an elephant-ear await.

As the gang meets at the river market and peruses the art, Bobby swings down to the other teriyaki cart to see if they have any go-ji left. They do. He didn’t note how they tasted as they went down, besides ‘fresh’ and cucumbery, but the real treat was how they helped soak up all the sugar from the donuts and the elephant-ear. Through the buzz of his sugar rush, Bobby remembers following the gang underneath a light dusting of rain back downtown through an otaku store where he hugged a cat-bus, which just so happened to be down the street from the blackened chicken teriyaki sandwich.

Moments later, having manipulated his gang into the food market like a herd of cats, Bobby finally gets to enjoy a moment with the subject of his desire. He admires the stack on the sandwich as it is placed on the table. He gives the sammy a squeeze and release and he marvels for a brief moment as it springs back to shape, the blackened breading gives a satisfying and juicy crunch. As the first bite hits his belly, Bobby’s eyes roll into the top of his head. He loses all memory before the moment he wakes up across town, holding a Rainier in one hand and a Chicago dog in the other, chawing on a dill pickle spear as though it were a cigar. He has apparently just lost a game of Connect Four.

“C’mon buddy” said Dylan. “Let’s get you home.” Dylan was taking a bag of Takis out of Bobby’s hands while trying to get him into his car.”

“Can you swing by Taco Bell on the way?” said Bobby as he nestled himself down into a squeaky faux-leather passenger seat.
